## Questions

### Q1

**Type:** Multiple choice

**Learner-facing question:** What is the main purpose of the quad chart presentation in Week 10?

A. To show as many equations as possible on one slide  
B. To communicate the full prediction-to-outcome logic, uncertainty analysis, and improvement argument in a compact, defensible format  
C. To avoid answering questions from the audience  
D. To replace the need for a group report

**Expert response:** B is the best answer. The quad chart is meant to condense the core analytical story of the experiment into a form that can be defended clearly under questioning.

### Q2

**Type:** Multiple choice

**Learner-facing question:** What makes a strong peer review comment in the Week 10 sense?

A. “Nice job”  
B. “This was confusing”  
C. A specific comment tied to the technical content, explaining what is correct, what is weak or missing, and what improvement would strengthen the work  
D. A comment that only focuses on slide colors

**Expert response:** C is the best answer. Week 10 continues the course theme that critique quality matters. Good feedback is specific, technical, and actionable.

### Q3

**Type:** Multiple choice

**Learner-facing question:** Why is E2 a bridge to E3?

A. Because after E2, the course no longer uses models  
B. Because E2 trains students to critique and improve existing experiments, which supports the later task of designing original experiments from scratch  
C. Because E3 is only about presentation style  
D. Because E2 removes the need for prediction

**Expert response:** B is the best answer. E2 strengthens the habits of prediction, comparison, critique, and improvement that become essential when students must create their own design in E3.

### Q4

**Type:** Open response

**Learner-facing question:** If someone challenges one of your presentation conclusions during the Q&A, what does Week 10 suggest you should defend first: the conclusion itself, the evidence behind it, or something else?

**Expert response:** A strong response says that the conclusion should be defended through the evidence and logic behind it: prediction, measurements, uncertainty treatment, comparison method, and interpretation. Week 10 expects students to defend claims by tracing the argument, not by restating the conclusion more confidently.

### Q5

**Type:** Open response

**Learner-facing question:** In one short paragraph, explain how the course’s idea of “good critique” is different from just being negative.

**Expert response:** A strong response explains that good critique identifies strengths, weaknesses, missing pieces, and specific improvements using technical evidence. It is constructive because its goal is to improve the engineering work, not to dismiss the person who produced it. That distinction has been built throughout the course and is especially visible in presentation peer review.
