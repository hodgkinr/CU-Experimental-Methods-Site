## Legend

`?` means the status is an informed inference from nearby planning artifacts or notes.

For readings and lectures:

- `D` = draft
- `R` = reviewed by a person
- `L` = lightboard recorded
- `E` = edited
- `C` = Canvas / ready for production

For assignments:

- `D` = draft
- `R` = reviewed by a person
- `L` = LaTeX / Overleaf ready
- `C` = Canvas / ready for production
- `A` = AI grader ready

Blank status means placeholder only.

## Notes

- `E1` readings and labs through `P=IV` and `Tier 1` are marked `R?` because `notes.txt` explicitly says those areas are "reviewed," but the note is broad rather than item-by-item.
- Most lectures are marked `D?` because the notes describe them as placeholders that are sufficient for now, which suggests substantial draft status but not clear evidence of recording, edit, or Canvas deployment.
- `E2` readings and assignments have verification and student-review artifacts nearby, but I did not treat that alone as proof of person-reviewed final status.
- The weekly `concept_quizzes` are marked `D?` because the artifacts now exist and include learner-facing questions, expert responses, and grader-prompt templates, but I have not seen evidence yet of human review, Canvas deployment, or live pipeline validation.
- I did not mark anything `L`, `E`, `C`, or `A` because I did not find solid evidence in the Planning repo or nearby notes that those later-stage milestones are complete.
- I did not invent `E3` lecture/reading/assignment rows because this `claude_V1` directory does not yet contain detailed E3 student-facing content analogous to E1 and E2.
