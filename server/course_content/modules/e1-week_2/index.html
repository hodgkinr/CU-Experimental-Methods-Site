E1 Week 2: Uncertainty Foundations: Power, Monte Carlo & MATLAB

Measurement produces variation. Week 2 gives students the mathematical and computational apparatus to characterize and communicate that variation rigorously. Learning Goals

  • Distinguish variability, measurement error, and uncertainty, and explain why the distinction matters for engineering claims — CLO 2
  • Apply first-order error propagation using partial derivatives to compute uncertainty in a derived quantity — CLO 2
  • Explain how different mathematical representations of the same physical quantity yield different sensitivities to measurement variation — CLO 2
  • Run a Monte Carlo simulation in MATLAB to model uncertainty in a derived quantity and interpret the resulting distribution — CLO 2
  • Use experimental results to inform and update a predictive model — not merely compare to it — and articulate what the data revealed about model limitations — CLO 3
  • Write a structured lab report with required sections: measurement procedure, error source classification, uncertainty propagation, confidence interval, MATLAB figures with captions, and a plain-language explanation accessible to a non-technical audience — CLO 5
