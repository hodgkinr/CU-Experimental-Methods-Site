Week 10 focus: Quad chart communication, defending analysis choices, peer review quality, and transition from improving existing experiments to designing new ones.

  1. Q1 Multiple Choice

    What is the main purpose of the quad chart presentation in Week 10?

    1. To show as many equations as possible on one slide
    2. To communicate the full prediction-to-outcome logic, uncertainty analysis, and improvement argument in a compact, defensible format
    3. To avoid answering questions from the audience
    4. To replace the need for a group report
  2. Q2 Multiple Choice

    What makes a strong peer review comment in the Week 10 sense?

    1. “Nice job”
    2. “This was confusing”
    3. A specific comment tied to the technical content, explaining what is correct, what is weak or missing, and what improvement would strengthen the work
    4. A comment that only focuses on slide colors
  3. Q3 Multiple Choice

    Why is E2 a bridge to E3?

    1. Because after E2, the course no longer uses models
    2. Because E2 trains students to critique and improve existing experiments, which supports the later task of designing original experiments from scratch
    3. Because E3 is only about presentation style
    4. Because E2 removes the need for prediction

    If someone challenges one of your presentation conclusions during the Q&A, what does Week 10 suggest you should defend first: the conclusion itself, the evidence behind it, or something else?
