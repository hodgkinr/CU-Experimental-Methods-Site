Questions

Week 5 focus: Prediction before data release, committed reasoning, discrepancy interpretation, and cross-prediction.

  1. Q1 Multiple Choice

    Why does the course require a prediction memo before students see the data?

    1. To make the assignment longer
    2. To force students to commit to reasoning before hindsight can reshape the story
    3. To prevent any use of uncertainty analysis
    4. To guarantee that every prediction is correct
  2. Q2 Multiple Choice

    Which of the following is the strongest prediction in the Week 5 sense?

    1. “The result will probably be close.”
    2. “The sensor should give reasonable data.”
    3. “The measured response should peak near X with uncertainty about Y, and we will consider agreement acceptable if the data falls within the committed bound.”
    4. “We will know whether the model is good after we look at the data.”
  3. Q3 Multiple Choice

    If a prediction is wrong but the reasoning was careful and committed in advance, what is the best Week 5 interpretation?

    1. The work was useless
    2. The student should hide the prediction and rewrite it
    3. The discrepancy is valuable because it can reveal model limitations or missing physics
    4. The experiment automatically failed
  4. Q4 Open Response

    Why is predicting someone else’s experiment from specs a useful training exercise, even though it is not the same as running the experiment yourself?

  5. Q5 Open Response

    A team says, “Our prediction was off, but that just means the experiment was bad.” What would Week 5 push you to ask next?
