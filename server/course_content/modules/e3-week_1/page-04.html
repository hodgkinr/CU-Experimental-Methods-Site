Learning Objectives

  • Apply formal experimental design vocabulary — independent and dependent variables, replication, randomization, blocking, control, and confounding factors — to an aerospace measurement problem — CLO 1
  • State a formal hypothesis that defines what a proposed experiment is expected to show, what data would confirm it, and what data would challenge it — CLO 1, CLO 3

Part 5 - Readiness Check

Before submitting, answer yes or revise.

  • [ ] The proposal has a model claim, not just a topic.
  • [ ] The response feature is measurable.
  • [ ] The hypothesis could be challenged by future data.
  • [ ] Confounders are specific to the physical setup.
  • [ ] The test matrix is small enough to defend and rich enough to teach something.
  • [ ] At least one relevant standard or professional guide is cited, and its relevance and limits are annotated.
  • [ ] At least one additional source contributes a design implication.
  • [ ] The proposal says how evidence would inform or improve the model.

Part 6 - One-Sentence Proposal

"I propose to test [model claim] by measuring [response feature] while varying [independent variable], controlling [key controls], using [initial measurement approach], so that the result can inform [intended use or model update]."
