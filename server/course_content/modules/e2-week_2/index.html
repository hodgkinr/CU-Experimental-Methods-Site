E2 Week 7: Signal Conditioning, Sampling & DAQ Fundamentals

Before a complex system can be measured reliably, its signals must be understood. Week 7 provides the foundational treatment of signal conditioning, sampling, and data acquisition that the canned aerospace experiments make concrete. Three core lecture topics anchor the week: Nyquist sampling and aliasing, analog signal conditioning and filtering principles, and DAQ configuration choices and noise sources. Students...

Learning Goals

  • Apply the Nyquist sampling theorem to identify the minimum sampling rate required for a given signal bandwidth, and predict the consequences of aliasing when the theorem is violated — CLO 4
  • Explain the purpose and design rationale of an anti-aliasing filter, and identify appropriate filter cutoff frequency relative to signal bandwidth and sampling rate — CLO 4
  • Identify and describe at least three common DAQ configuration choices — channel configuration, resolution, sample rate — and articulate the tradeoff each choice imposes — CLO 4
  • Visualize a measured signal in both time and frequency domain using MATLAB and identify signal bandwidth from the frequency-domain representation — CLO 4
  • Produce a DAQ configuration justification section in the Tier 2 group report documenting sampling frequency selection, filtering rationale, and noise source identification with MATLAB signal visualizations — CLO 4, CLO 5
  • Read and interpret DAQ configuration documentation for a real instrument, identifying channel configuration, resolution, and sample rate choices and articulating the tradeoff each imposes — CLO 4
