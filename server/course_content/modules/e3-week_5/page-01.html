AMVF Mapping and Reflection Worksheet

A reading-format worksheet that scaffolds the W15 AMVF mapping, CLO 6 reflection, course reflection, and final answer to the course-opening question. Students trace one engineering decision across E1, E2, and E3; map it to AMVF nodes; document the beyond-provided method, sensor, platform, standard, or source they investigated; explain whether AI-assisted claims were verified; and use concrete evidence to describe how their understanding of uncertainty, prediction, validation, and experimental design changed. ## 3. Bridge to Next Week There is no Week 16. The course ends here — but the capability it developed does not. Students carry forward a structured, repeatable method for earning confidence in a prediction: design the experiment, specify the instrumentation, quantify the uncertainty, define the acceptance criterion, commit before measuring, compare honestly, and use the results to improve the model. That method has no expiration date.

The course ends by asking what changed in your engineering judgment

The final reflection is not a victory lap; it is evidence that you now think differently about experiments.

Week 15 closes ASEN 3501 by returning to the question that started the course: how do you know this prediction is right? Your answer should be more disciplined now. It should include a model claim, a measurement strategy, uncertainty, a validation metric, intended use, and a willingness to revise the model when evidence demands it. Use this worksheet to map that growth and to prepare your CLO 6 and course reflections.
