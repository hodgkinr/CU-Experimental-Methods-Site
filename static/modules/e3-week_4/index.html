E3 Week 4 (Course Week 14)

E3 Week 14: Oral Defense, Communication & Peer Assessment

Technical credibility is only as good as its communication. Week 14 centers on oral defense of the V&V plans developed in Week 13: students present their experimental design, instrumentation rationale, uncertainty analysis, and validation plan to the class in a formal presentation setting. Audience members — both peers and the instructor — provide structured written evaluations that assess the presentation on spec...

Learning Goals

  • Deliver a structured oral defense of an experimental design and V&V plan to a technical audience, responding to questions about instrumentation choices, uncertainty analysis, and validation logic — CLO 5
  • Evaluate a peer's experimental design using a structured rubric, identifying specific strengths, genuine weaknesses, and concrete improvement recommendations — CLO 5, CLO 6
  • Apply technical communication principles — structured arguments, figures as arguments, results separated from interpretation, limitations stated explicitly — in the oral and written communication of experimental design work — CLO 5
  • Analyze sample experimental design work — from prior course iterations, professional reports, or AI-generated content — using the same evaluation rubric applied to peer presentations, demonstrating that critique quality is independent of the source of the work being evaluated — CLO 5, CLO 6
