E2 Week 1 (Course Week 6)

E2 Week 6: Prediction Before Exposure & Formal Hypothesis Framing

The defining epistemic move of E2 is introduced here: prediction must precede measurement, and that sequence is not arbitrary. Week 6 builds directly on the cross-prediction exercise of Week 5 and formalizes the idea that committing to a hypothesis before encountering data is not just good scientific practice — it is the standard of engineering validation. Learning Goals

  • Explain why postdiction — fitting a model to data already collected — is fundamentally different from genuine validation, and identify cognitive biases that make postdiction feel like validation — CLO 3
  • Formulate a formal hypothesis for a complex aerospace system, specifying expected system response features and acceptable agreement criteria before any data is released — CLO 1, CLO 3
  • Define intended use as the primary framing constraint on all predictive modeling decisions — CLO 3
  • Submit a pre-experiment prediction document for a canned aerospace experiment with reasoning that is graded independently of whether the prediction is correct — CLO 1, CLO 5
  • Connect the hypothesis-first discipline of E1's simple experiments to the prediction-first discipline of E2's complex system experiments — CLO 1, CLO 3
  • Articulate how experimental results can be used to inform and update a predictive model — not merely validate it — as the stated purpose of every E2 canned experiment — CLO 3
