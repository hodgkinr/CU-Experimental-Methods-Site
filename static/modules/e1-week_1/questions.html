Questions

Week 1 focus: AMVF, verification vs. validation, measurement vs. inference, and formal experiment design.

  1. Q1 Multiple Choice

    In the course framing, what is the main job of validation?

    1. Checking whether the experiment was performed safely
    2. Checking whether the equations were solved correctly
    3. Checking whether the model is an adequate representation of the real physical system
    4. Checking whether the sensor was calibrated correctly
  2. Q2 Multiple Choice

    Which statement best captures the difference between what a sensor measures and what an engineer often cares about?

    1. A sensor always measures the final engineering quantity directly.
    2. A sensor measures a raw signal, and the engineering quantity is often inferred through a calibration or model.
    3. A sensor only measures noise, and the engineer removes it later.
    4. A sensor and a model are unrelated parts of the workflow.
  3. Q3 Multiple Choice

    Which hypothesis is the most testable in the Week 1 sense?

    1. “The system will probably change when we run the experiment.”
    2. “We expect interesting behavior.”
    3. “If input voltage is increased from 3 V to 9 V while resistance is held constant, measured power will increase by a factor of nine because power scales with V^2/R.”
    4. “The data should make sense if we do the procedure correctly.”
  4. Q4 Open Response

    Explain the difference between verification and validation in your own words, and give one short engineering-style example of each.

  5. Q5 Open Response

    A team says their sensor reads force directly. Based on Week 1, what would you want to ask before accepting that claim?
