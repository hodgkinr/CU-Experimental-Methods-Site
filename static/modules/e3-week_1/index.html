E3 Week 1 (Course Week 11)

E3 Week 11: Experimental Design Principles, Hypothesis Formulation & Prior Art

E3 opens by formalizing something students have been doing implicitly since Week 1: making decisions about what to measure, how many times, and under what conditions. Week 11 makes experimental design explicit — independent and dependent variables, replication, randomization, control, confounding factors, and a structured introduction to design of experiments concepts at the exposure level. Learning Goals

  • Apply formal experimental design vocabulary — independent and dependent variables, replication, randomization, blocking, control, and confounding factors — to an aerospace measurement problem — CLO 1
  • State a formal hypothesis that defines what a proposed experiment is expected to show, what data would confirm it, and what data would challenge it — CLO 1, CLO 3
  • Identify and cite ASME V&V 10-2006 and at least one additional relevant engineering standard, handbook, or prior publication applicable to their E3 design challenge — CLO 1, CLO 6
  • Describe what is already established in prior literature about their measurement domain and articulate what gap or question their experiment addresses — CLO 6
  • Frame the purpose of the E3 design challenge explicitly: the experiment exists to inform and improve a defined predictive model — not merely to validate it in a binary sense — CLO 3
  • Produce a preliminary experimental design proposal including problem statement, hypothesis, variable identification, relevant standards cited, and prior art summary — CLO 1, CLO 5, CLO 6
