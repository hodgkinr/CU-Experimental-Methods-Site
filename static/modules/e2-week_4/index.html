E2 Week 9: Statistical Comparison of Datasets with MATLAB & Monte Carlo

Week 9 completes the Phase II analytical toolkit by extending quantitative comparison to full datasets and repeated measurements. Students encounter hypothesis testing at a conceptual level, compare means and confidence interval overlap, apply regression and goodness-of-fit measures to assess model adequacy, and use Monte Carlo simulation in MATLAB to propagate uncertainty through complex derived quantities. Learning Goals

  • Compare means and confidence intervals from multiple experimental datasets and distinguish statistical significance from engineering significance — CLO 2
  • Apply regression analysis to a dataset and interpret the goodness-of-fit measures in terms of model adequacy — CLO 2, CLO 3
  • Use Monte Carlo simulation in MATLAB to propagate uncertainty through a multi-variable aerospace system and interpret the resulting distribution — CLO 2
  • Identify systematic trends in residuals across multiple trials and articulate their implication for model adequacy — CLO 3
  • Use experimental results from multiple conditions to propose a specific improvement to the predictive model — connecting statistical patterns in residuals to model revision decisions — CLO 3
  • Apply these tools directly to the Tier 2 group report uncertainty analysis and improvement proposal sections — CLO 2, CLO 5
