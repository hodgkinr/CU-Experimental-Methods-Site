AI Literacy vs. AI Fluency

AI literacy is knowing what AI is and what it can do. AI fluency is knowing when, how, and why to use it, and when not to. This course expects fluency, not just literacy.

Creation is now cheap. The durable skill is evaluation: deciding what is correct, what is weak, what is missing, and how to make it better. That is why ASEN 3501 keeps returning to critique, validation, and defended engineering judgment.

Why This Course Uses Authentic Assessments

As AI gets better, the assessments that still reveal real understanding are the ones AI cannot take for you: live explanation, in-class reasoning, oral review, and design defense.

That is why the backbone of this course is not "produce more words." It is "defend what those words mean."

The Employer Question

One question is worth carrying through the entire semester:

How will this task make me a valuable asset to a future employer?

The point is not to avoid foundational work because AI can do parts of it. The point is to use the foundational work to become the engineer who can judge whether the AI-assisted result is any good.

A Practical Way to Use AI in This Course

  • use AI to get unstuck on mechanics
  • use AI to surface alternative explanations or literature leads
  • use AI to critique a draft before submission
  • do not hand over final judgment on correctness, scope, or engineering adequacy

If AI gives you something plausible, your job is still to ask: is it right, what evidence supports it, and what would make me distrust it?
